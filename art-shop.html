A Brief History of the Stick — Ian

The walking stick didn't start as a symbol of weakness. It started as a symbol of power — the authority to speak to God, to channel a deity, to hold the room. Kings and aristocrats took it as a trophy. Landlords used it to collect rent from the working class. Literally.

Then the men came back from World War II — disabled, proud, and suddenly holding a thing that Hollywood decided made you the villain. The guy who tied the woman to the railway tracks. The bitter cripple. Disability became the backstory for why someone hated the world, instead of the world failing to meet their needs.

The stick went from staff of God to stigma in a few decades. Not because the stick changed. Because the narrative did.

GenXcess is the correction. We own the stick. The stick doesn't own us.

It should be a handbag. A pair of shoes. An expression of who you are — with extra function. A Bluetooth speaker. A wand. A cane with attitude and theme music. That's what it was always supposed to be.
